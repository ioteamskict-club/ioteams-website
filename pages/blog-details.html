DIGITEX 2026: Exploring the Future of Digital Innovation

IoTeams IIUM secured Kulliyyah approval and headed to UTM Johor Bahru for the Digital Innovation & Technology Exhibition (DIGITEX) 2026 — a platform bringing together students, academics, researchers, and industry professionals to explore advancements in digital innovation, engineering, and emerging technologies.

Wan Zul 'Irfan
President · IoTeams IIUM
⭐ Featured

Six members, one advisor, and a green light from the Kulliyyah — that's all it took for IoTeams IIUM to earn a seat at DIGITEX 2026. Here's the story of how we got there, what the competition actually involves, and what's coming up next.

The Digital Innovation & Technology Exhibition (DIGITEX) 2026, organised by the Faculty of Computing at Universiti Teknologi Malaysia (UTM), brought together students, academics, researchers, and industry professionals from across the region to showcase ideas in web and mobile development, hardware and software innovation, and IoT-based connected systems. IoTeams represented the Kulliyyah of Information and Communication Technology (KICT), IIUM, at the Grand Finale held in Johor Bahru.

Organiser Faculty of Computing, UTM
Grand Finale 16 June 2026
Venue N28a, Faculty of Computing, UTM JB
IoTeams Squad 6 Students + 1 Advisor

What Is DIGITEX 2026?

DIGITEX 2026 serves as a platform that brings together students, academics, researchers, and industry professionals to explore advancements in digital innovation, engineering, and emerging technologies. The programme aims to foster collaboration and encourage the development of creative technological solutions to real-world challenges.

Beyond the competition format, DIGITEX promotes knowledge exchange and professional networking, giving participants a space to showcase their projects, gain exposure to current technological trends, and receive feedback directly from industry experts and judges — while representing their home institutions on an inter-university stage.

  • Web & Mobile Application — apps and platforms solving real-world problems.
  • Hardware & Software Innovation — integrated builds spanning both disciplines.
  • Internet of Things (IoT) Devices & Connected Systems — sensors, automation, and connected hardware.
  • Other Related Technologies & Devices Prototypes — open-category emerging tech.

Showing up at DIGITEX wasn't just about the pitch on finale day — it was weeks of paperwork, rehearsal, and late-night fixes that got us onto that stage in the first place.

Getting the Green Light

Before any team can represent IIUM externally, the Kulliyyah has to formally sign off on it. UTM's Faculty of Computing first extended an invitation to KICT in late March, encouraging students to take part and promote the programme. From there, our advisor prepared a working paper outlining the objectives, schedule, and budget for participation, which was routed through the Department of Computer Science and the Kulliyyah for approval.

By 21 April 2026, the Kulliyyah of Information and Communication Technology had officially approved the proposal — appointing Ts. Dr. Ahmad Anwar bin Zainuddin as advisor and allocating budget support from the Student Operating Trust to cover participation costs. With that approval in hand, the real preparation began.

Behind the scenes: Every external competition IoTeams enters goes through this same approval chain — advisor sign-off, working paper, Kulliyyah review — before a single registration form gets submitted. It's not the most glamorous part of competing, but it's what makes the trip possible.

For IoTeams specifically, that meant a tight internal timeline too — registering and submitting our video before mid-May, refining the pitch once we were confirmed as finalists, and finalising the poster ahead of the 10 June deadline.

Exhibition Topics & Awards

DIGITEX welcomed entries from a wide range of participants — public and private agencies, institutions of higher learning, and even primary and secondary schools, with separate categories for local and international entrants. Finalists who made it to the Grand Finale competed for:

AwardPrize
Best of the BestRM2,000 + Plaque + Certificate
Best AwardRM1,000 + Plaque + Certificate
Gold, Silver & BronzeMedal + Certificate (Top 3 Winners)

Our team's submission fell under the IoT Devices & Connected Systems track — a natural fit given the kind of connected hardware projects IoTeams builds on campus throughout the year.

Grand Finale Day at UTM Johor Bahru

The Grand Finale took place on Tuesday, 16 June 2026, at N28a, Faculty of Computing, UTM Johor Bahru, running from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Finalists from across institutions set up posters, demoed working prototypes, and pitched their projects in front of judges and fellow participants.

For our squad, the day was the payoff for months of preparation — from the first approval letter back in April, through the registration window, the finalist announcement, and the poster deadline, all the way to standing in front of a panel and defending the work in person.

Per the competition terms, travel and accommodation for finalists were covered by the participants or their respective faculty — a good reminder to budget early if your team makes it through to a Grand Finale.

What's Next for IoTeams

DIGITEX 2026 wraps up our latest external competition, but the momentum doesn't stop here. A programme report and financial report are due back to the Kulliyyah within two weeks of completion, and we're already looking ahead to what's next:

  1. Submit our reports — programme and financial reports back to KICT, as required by the approval terms.
  2. Debrief as a team — what worked in the pitch, what we'd refine for the next competition.
  3. Keep building — carry the IoT project forward beyond the exhibition stage.
  4. Open the next call — start recruiting for the next competition cycle.

Want to be part of the next team that represents IoTeams on stage? Keep an eye on our socials and project demo days — that's usually where the next squad gets put together.
