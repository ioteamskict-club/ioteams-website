Silver at Home, Top 10 Abroad: IoTeams at NSVIC & TeknoCom 2026

Two competitions, one packed season. IoTeams took home a Silver Award at the National Virtual STEM Innovation Competition (NSVIC) 2026, then sent ten teams into TeknoCom 2026 — Universitas Teknokrat Indonesia's international Scientific Paper competition — with seven of them reaching the Top 10.

Wan Nurqaisara Damia binti Wan Mohd Adnan

Two competitions, six weeks apart, one very full season. While one squad was pitching a smart-home security concept to a national panel, ten more teams were drafting scientific papers for an international stage in Indonesia. Here's how both went.

This recap covers two separate competitions IoTeams entered back to back: the National Virtual STEM Innovation Competition (NSVIC) 2026, organised by Persatuan Mahasiswa Sains (PMS) at Universiti Pendidikan Sultan Idris (UPSI) under the STEM Innovation & Career Ecosystem (STEM-ICE) initiative; and TeknoCom 2026, an international Scientific Paper competition organised by the Fakultas Teknik & Ilmu Komputer at Universitas Teknokrat Indonesia. Both ran fully online — NSVIC from 27 April to 17 June 2026, and TeknoCom from 13 March to 20 June 2026.

NSVIC 2026: Innovating for a Sustainable Future

The National Virtual STEM Innovation Competition 2026 is a national platform for students to showcase creative STEM solutions. The fully online event ran under the official theme "Innovating for a Sustainable, Intelligent and Inclusive Future," with IoTeams competing in Category D: Digital Technology and AI.

The objective wasn't just to enter — the team set out to design a meaningful STEM solution addressing real-world challenges, sharpen technical writing and concise digital communication, and benchmark the result against national standards through professional and academic evaluation.

The team's entry, TrustLock AI — a decentralised smart access system concept for next-generation homes — earned IoTeams a Silver Award at the virtual Closing Ceremony on 17 June 2026, on top of producing a complete technical project paper and a polished 3-minute pitch video.

  • Future-Ready Mindset — sharper problem-solving for the demands of a fast-moving tech industry.
  • Societal & Industrial Relevance — a tangible concept aimed at real community and industrial needs.
  • STEM Exposure — direct exposure to the national STEM ecosystem, setting a benchmark for future entries.

TeknoCom 2026: Ten Teams, One International Stage

TeknoCom 2026 is an international competition organised by the Fakultas Teknik & Ilmu Komputer at Universitas Teknokrat Indonesia, themed "Next-Generation Technologies for Global Challenges." It ran virtually from 13 March to 20 June 2026. IoTeams didn't send one team — we sent ten, all competing in the Scientific Paper category, each paired with Ts. Dr. Ahmad Anwar bin Zainuddin and fellow KICT academics as co-authors and mentors.

Results: Top 10 Finalists

Registration and proposal submission ran from 13 March to an extended deadline of 3 June 2026. The official Top 10 Scientific Paper Finalist list was announced on 8 June, followed by a mandatory Technical Briefing on 10 June to finalise presentation guidelines.

When the global Top 10 Scientific Paper Finalist list dropped, seven of those ten spots belonged to IoTeams teams.

Teams 1, 2, 3, 4, 7, 9, and 10 all qualified as Top 10 Finalists — competing against entries from across the region for a place on Competition Day. The live virtual Competition Day ran on 17 June 2026, with the official Awarding Ceremony scheduled for 20 June 2026.

As of writing, the Awarding Ceremony hasn't taken place yet — this post will be updated once final placings for the Scientific Paper category are announced.

What We Learned

NSVIC reinforced that high-impact innovation lives or dies on concise, value-driven communication — condensing a technical project into a strict 3-minute video meant prioritising clarity over complexity, and drafting the project paper hammered home the importance of structured documentation and aligning a solution with real-world relevance.

TeknoCom raised the bar further. Competing on an international stage meant balancing advanced technical engineering with intuitive, universally accessible design, and articulating that work confidently in front of a diverse global panel. Across both competitions, the same thread kept coming up: tight submission timelines reward agile project management and disciplined milestone tracking far more than last-minute heroics.

What's Next for IoTeams

With NSVIC closed out and TeknoCom's Awarding Ceremony just around the corner, the team is moving straight into wrap-up mode:

  1. Watch the TeknoCom results — the Awarding Ceremony lands on 20 June 2026.
  2. Document everything — consolidate this season's reports while the lessons are still fresh.
  3. Debrief as eleven teams — compare notes across every squad that competed this season.
  4. Carry TrustLock AI forward — explore turning the Silver Award concept into a working prototype.
